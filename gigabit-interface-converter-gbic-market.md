<p><p>Gigabit Interface Converters (GBICs) are modular transceivers used in networking for connecting different types of fiber optic and copper cables. The market for GBICs is segmented into singlemode and multimode categories. Singlemode GBICs support longer distance connections, ideal for wide area networks, utilizing a single light path. In contrast, multimode GBICs are designed for shorter distances and higher data rates, suitable for local area networks. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing network flexibility and performance.</p></p>
