<p><p>The cultivated meat cell culture media market is categorized into serum-free and serum-containing media. Serum-free media are designed without animal-derived components, promoting ethical production methods while minimizing contamination risks. They are often tailored for specific cell types, enhancing growth and productivity. In contrast, serum-containing media include components such as fetal bovine serum, which provide necessary growth factors and nutrients but raise ethical and supply chain concerns. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing cell growth for cultivated meat production.</p></p>
