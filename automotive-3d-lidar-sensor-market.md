<p><p>The automotive 3D LiDAR sensor market is primarily segmented into mechanical and solid-state LiDAR types. Mechanical LiDAR employs rotating components to capture a wide field of view, providing high-resolution 3D mapping and object detection. It is known for its accuracy but can be more complex and expensive. In contrast, solid-state LiDAR utilizes fixed, miniaturized components, resulting in higher durability, lower production costs, and easier integration into vehicles. Solid-state options are becoming increasingly popular for their reliability in diverse driving conditions.</p></p>
