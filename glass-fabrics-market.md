<p><p>The glass fabrics market offers various weave types, each serving distinct applications. Plain weave is the most common, providing a balanced structure and versatility. Leno weave features a unique interlocking pattern, enhancing stability and reducing fraying, ideal for architectural uses. Twill weave, characterized by diagonal lines, offers strength and flexibility, making it suitable for automotive and aerospace applications. Satin weave has a smooth, lustrous finish, offering aesthetic appeal and used in high-performance applications, emphasizing both beauty and functionality.</p></p>
