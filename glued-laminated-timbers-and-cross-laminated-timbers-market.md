<p><p>Glued Laminated Timbers (GLT) and Cross Laminated Timbers (CLT) are engineered wood products made from multiple layers of timber bonded together with adhesives for enhanced strength and stability. GLT typically consists of layers arranged parallel to the length of the beam, making it ideal for structural applications like beams and columns. In contrast, CLT features layers of wood arranged perpendicular to each other, providing greater dimensional stability and making it suitable for walls, floors, and roofs in multi-story buildings.</p><p>The market for GLT and CLT has been experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for sustainable building materials and the rising focus on green construction practices. These timber products offer advantages such as lower carbon footprints, reduced waste, and excellent thermal insulation properties.
